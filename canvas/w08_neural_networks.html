Midterm 1 & Neural Network Fundamentals

We close the classical half of the course and open the second: learning representations.

📝 Midterm 1 (Lecture 1)

Pen-and-paper, 2 hours, one A4 sheet (recto-verso) allowed. Scope: everything up to W7: EDA, ARIMA, regression, classification, SVMs, cross-validation, trees, ensembles, clustering, PCA. No neural networks. Best of the two midterms counts.

Overview

After the midterm we begin neural networks. A network is just stacked linear layers with non-linear activations, trained by gradient descent and backpropagation. Everything from earlier weeks still applies: overfitting, the bias–variance tradeoff, train/test discipline, but now the model learns its own features. We build the multilayer perceptron from the ground up and train one on MNIST.

By the end of this week you can…
  • Describe a feed-forward network: layers, weights, activations, forward pass.
  • Explain how gradient descent and backprop update the weights.
  • Choose activation functions and reason about vanishing gradients.
  • Build and train an MLP in PyTorch and combat overfitting (dropout, optimizers).
