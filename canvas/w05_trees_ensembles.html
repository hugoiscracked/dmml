Week 05 · Tree-Based Models

Trees & Ensembles

From one interpretable tree to a forest that votes.

Overview

Decision trees split feature space into rectangles with rules a human can read; but a single deep tree overfits badly. The fix is ensembles: bagging many decorrelated trees into a Random Forest that is far more robust than any of its members. We cover splitting criteria, pruning, feature importance, and why averaging works.

By the end of this week you can…
  • Build and read a decision tree, and explain its splitting criterion.
  • Diagnose tree overfitting and control it (depth, pruning, min-samples).
  • Explain bagging and why decorrelated trees reduce variance.
  • Train a Random Forest and interpret feature importances (and their caveats).
