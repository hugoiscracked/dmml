Week 03 · Supervised Learning

Regression & Classification Basics

The supervised frame, the bias–variance tradeoff, and the metrics that keep us honest.

Overview

This is the conceptual heart of the course. We introduce supervised learning — learning a function f(X) ≈ y — starting with the most interpretable models: linear and polynomial regression, then logistic regression and k-NN for classification. Along the way we meet the bias–variance tradeoff (which returns every single week from now on) and the train/test discipline that separates real ML from self-deception. We close with a careful tour of classification metrics, because accuracy alone lies.

By the end of this week you can…
  • Fit and interpret linear and polynomial regression, and choose the right error metric.
  • Explain the bias–variance tradeoff and connect it to under/overfitting.
  • Train logistic regression and k-NN, and explain what each assumes.
  • Build a confusion matrix and compute precision, recall, F1, ROC/AUC.
  • Say why accuracy is misleading under class imbalance.
