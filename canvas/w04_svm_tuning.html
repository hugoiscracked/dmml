Week 04 · Kernels & Validation

SVMs & Model Tuning

Max-margin classifiers, the kernel trick, and hyperparameters tuning.

Overview

Support Vector Machines find the boundary with the widest possible margin, and the kernel trick lets them draw non-linear boundaries. The second half is methodology that applies to every model: cross-validation, grid search, pipelines, and avoiding data leakage.

By the end of this week you can…
  • Explain the max-margin idea and the role of support vectors.
  • Use the kernel trick (linear vs RBF) for non-linear classification.
  • Run k-fold cross-validation and grid search correctly.
  • Build leak-proof pipelines (fit the scaler on training data only).
  • Submit a SLURM batch job and retrieve the results.

In motion

The margin. The widest street between classes, defined by the support vectors.

The kernel trick. Lifting data into a higher dimension where it becomes linearly separable.

Cross-validation. Rotating the validation fold to use all the data without leaking.
