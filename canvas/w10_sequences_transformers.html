Sequences & Transformers

From recurrence and memory to attention — the architecture behind modern AI.

Overview

The technical finale. We start with RNNs and LSTMs, which process sequences step by step and carry a hidden state — powerful, but hampered by vanishing gradients. Then the leap: attention lets a model look directly at any part of the input, and the Transformer builds entirely on it, dropping recurrence for parallelism. This is the architecture behind the large language models reshaping the field; we close by fine-tuning one.

By the end of this week you can…
  • Explain how an RNN carries state, and why LSTMs add gates.
  • Describe the attention mechanism and self-attention.
  • Sketch the Transformer encoder: QKV, multi-head attention, residuals, positional encoding.
  • Fine-tune a pre-trained transformer for text classification.
