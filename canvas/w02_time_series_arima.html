Week 02 · Temporal Data

Time Series with ARIMA

When observations are ordered in time, the IID assumption dies — and everything changes.

Overview

Last week every row was independent. This week we break that assumption: in a time series, each observation depends on the ones before it. That single fact reshapes how we describe data (trend, seasonality, stationarity), how we split it (never shuffle!), and how we model it. We build up to ARIMA — the classical workhorse for forecasting — piece by piece (AR + I + MA), fit it, forecast, and critically evaluate the result on the classic airline-passengers dataset.

By the end of this week you can…
  • Decompose a series into trend, seasonality, and residual.
  • Diagnose stationarity (ADF/KPSS, visual) and fix it with differencing.
  • Read ACF/PACF plots to identify AR and MA structure.
  • Fit an ARIMA model, check residuals, and produce interval forecasts.
  • Split and validate time series correctly (walk-forward, never shuffle).
