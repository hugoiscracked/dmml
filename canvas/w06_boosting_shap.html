Week 06 · Boosting & Interpretability

Boosting & Advanced Tree Models

Boosting, or building a model that learns from its predecessors.

Overview

Where bagging averages independent trees, boosting grows them sequentially, each one correcting the errors of the last. Gradient boosting and its fast implementations XGBoost, LightGBM, and CatBoost dominate tabular ML competitions and industry alike. We mitigate the interpretability lost with SHAP, a way to attribute a prediction to its features.

By the end of this week you can…
  • Explain how boosting differs from bagging (sequential vs parallel; bias vs variance).
  • Train and tune XGBoost / LightGBM and know their key knobs.
  • Compare a Random Forest against a boosted model fairly.
  • Use SHAP values to explain individual predictions and global importance.
