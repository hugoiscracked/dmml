Week 09 · Deep Learning for Images

Training Techniques & CNNs

The tricks that make deep nets train and the architecture that conquered images.

Overview

First, the training toolkit that makes deep networks actually converge: dropout, batch normalization, Adam, learning-rate schedules, early stopping, and transfer learning. Second, convolutional neural networks, which exploit the spatial structure of images through shared filters and pooling to learn a hierarchy of features, from edges to objects.

By the end of this week you can…
  • Apply dropout, batch norm, Adam, and LR schedules, and say what each fixes.
  • Explain convolution, pooling, and why CNNs beat MLPs on images.
  • Build and train a CNN, and read its learning curves.
  • Fine-tune a pre-trained backbone via transfer learning.

CNN architecture. The full pipeline, hierarchical features, and transfer-learning freeze/unfreeze.

🖥️ Cluster: GPUs this week

CNN training on CPU is painful; this is what the cluster's GPUs are for. Request one with --gres=gpu:1 and always verify it's actually being used as PyTorch silently falls back to CPU otherwise:

#SBATCH --gres=gpu:1
nvidia-smi                  # GPU visible in the log?
python -c "import torch; assert torch.cuda.is_available()"

For debugging: srun --gres=gpu:1 --pty bash. GPUs are the most contested resource.
