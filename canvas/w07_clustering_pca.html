Week 07 · Unsupervised Learning

Clustering & Dimensionality Reduction

Finding structure when there are no labels — and seeing high-dimensional data in 2D.

Overview

We drop the labels. Clustering (K-means, DBSCAN, hierarchical) groups similar points; PCA compresses many correlated features into a few informative directions. Together they power exploratory analysis, segmentation, and visualisation — and PCA quietly improves many of the supervised methods from earlier weeks. We also confront the curse of dimensionality that motivates all of this.

By the end of this week you can…
  • Run K-means and choose K (elbow, silhouette) — and name its assumptions.
  • Use DBSCAN for non-spherical clusters and outliers.
  • Apply PCA, read the explained-variance curve, and project to 2D.
  • Distinguish feature selection from feature extraction.
